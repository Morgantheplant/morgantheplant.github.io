ABOUT: To help with my sunglass and watch purchases I was instructed to build a model stock for each of my vendors for each category. Since I was dealing with over 30+ vendors/classes in these two categories alone, I decided to build a single sheet that would take the raw sales data and update everything with a single copy and paste. Similar to the Best Seller Worksheet, this sheet pulls out and ranks relevant info depending on two user defined variables for vendor and class. Then on a separate tab this info is tallied and run through several calculations to determine how much money can be spent per vendor in terms of dollars and units (i.e. OTB). After completing this worksheet I could check stock and send sales reports with only a couple of clicks of the mouse.

FORMAT: The first tab [Past Sales Report HERE (a-v)] includes an area to paste your raw sales data at the start of the week. Similar to the Best Seller Worksheet, the adjacent columns to the right check each row and displays a SKU and PW Sales if it matches the vendor and class.

On the second tab [RAW] all of the SKUs displayed on the first tab are then sorted and ranked by PW sales. Instead of ranking top 15 like the Best Seller Worksheet, this tab counts all SKUs and uses this number to rank by top nth SKUs effectively pulling in everything with or without sales. The result is a snapshot of all SKUs, the current on hand, and recent sales data for each vendor + class.

The third tab [SALES] contains all sales data for each vendor + class for last fiscal year. Totals from the second tab are automatically pulled into this sheet to compare current on hand vs. suggested AMI/stock turnover for all brands. This gives a top down view of all categories and helps quickly see exactly we are over or under in planned stock.

The forth tab [STOCK PLAN] takes data from the second and third tab and breaks it into finer detail for each vendor. The result is a report that can be easily converted to values and sent to vendors to highlights true OTB in terms of units and dollars.
